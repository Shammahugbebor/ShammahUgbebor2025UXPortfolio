Introduction.

Optimizing End-of-Line Packaging with Collaborative Palletizer Systems

Palletizing, the process of stacking cases or products onto pallets for efficient storage and shipping, is a critical step in end-of-line packaging operations. Traditionally, this process has often been manual, leading to various challenges for manufacturers.

This case study explores the benefits and impact of implementing collaborative palletizer systems to automate this crucial task. It examines the common challenges associated with manual palletizing, the advantages of collaborative robotic solutions, and the overall transformation of end-of-line packaging processes.

Challenges of Manual Palletizing

1. Labor Costs and Availability: Manual palletizing is labor-intensive, requiring a significant number of workers. Finding and retaining employees for these physically demanding jobs can be difficult, leading to labor shortages and high turnover rates.

2. Ergonomic Risks and Worker Safety: The repetitive lifting, bending, and twisting involved in manual palletizing pose a high risk of musculoskeletal disorders and other workplace injuries. This can lead to increased worker compensation costs and decreased employee morale.

3. Operational Inefficiencies: Manual palletizing can be a bottleneck in the production process, limiting throughput and potentially causing downtime. Inconsistencies in stacking can also lead to unstable pallets and product damage during shipping.

4. Limited Flexibility and Scalability: Manual palletizing may struggle to adapt to changes in production volume, product size, or pallet configurations. Scaling up operations can also be challenging due to the need for additional labor.

How does it work?

A simple yet engaging gameplay loop that brings people together.

Collaborative palletizer systems automate the process of stacking cases or products onto pallets. These systems typically utilize a collaborative robot (cobot) equipped with an end-of-arm tool (EOAT), such as a vacuum gripper, to pick up cases from a conveyor. The cobot is programmed to follow specific palletizing patterns, precisely placing each case onto the pallet. The system often includes sensors and safety mechanisms that allow the cobot to work safely alongside human workers without the need for traditional safety guarding.

Operators can easily program and adjust pallet configurations through the system's software interface. The collaborative nature of these systems allows for a flexible and adaptable approach to palletizing, combining the precision and efficiency of automation with the adaptability of human oversight

User Feedback

User feedback on collaborative palletizer systems is generally positive, with key themes emerging

Relief from Physical Strain: Workers appreciate the reduction in physical strain and the elimination of the demanding tasks associated with manual palletizing.

Ease of Use: Operators find the systems relatively easy to learn and operate, with intuitive software interfaces.

Increased Productivity: Users observe a significant increase in palletizing speed and overall productivity.

Improved Safety: Workers feel safer in the work environment due to the reduced risk of injuries.

Positive Impact on Job Roles: Employees value the opportunity to take on more technical and skilled roles within the company.

Desire for More Advanced Features: Some users express a desire for more advanced features, such as data logging, reporting, and predictive maintenance capabilities.

Key Metrics

  • Throughput: Measured in pallets per hour or cases per hour. Increased throughput from ten (10) pallets per hour to fifteen (15) pallets per hour. Increased throughput from five hundred (500) cases per hour to seven hundred and fifty (750) cases per hour.
  • Cycle Time: The time taken to complete one palletizing cycle. Reduced cycle time from one hundred and twenty (120) seconds to ninety (90) seconds.
  • Downtime: The amount of time the system is not operational due to errors or maintenance. Reduced downtime from eight (8) hours per week to two (2) hours per week.
  • Injury Rates: The number of workplace injuries related to palletizing. Reduced injury rates from three (3) incidents per year to zero (0) incidents per year.
  • Error Rates: The frequency of errors in the palletizing process. Reduced error rates from five percent (5%) to one percent (1%).

What Next

  • The future of collaborative palletizer systems is focused on further enhancing their capabilities and expanding their applications. Based on ongoing research, stakeholder interviews, field studies, support call analysis, and user/company feedback, key areas of development include
  • Enhance AI-powered optimization of pallet patterns.
  • Integration with autonomous mobile robots (AMRs) for seamless material flow.
  • Advance data analytics and predictive maintenance capabilities.
  • Improve software interfaces for even easier programming and operation.
  • Focus on sustainability through energy-efficient designs and reduced waste.
